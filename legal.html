1. FTC Credit Repair Organizations Act (CROA) Disclosure

IMPORTANT INFORMATION ABOUT YOUR RIGHTS UNDER THE CREDIT REPAIR ORGANIZATIONS ACT

The Credit Repair Organizations Act requires all credit repair organizations to provide you with a copy of the "Consumer Rights" statement before you are obligated to pay for credit repair services.

Your Consumer Rights

You have the right to:

  1. Sue a credit repair organization that violates the Credit Repair Organizations Act or state credit repair laws within two years from the date of the violation
  2. Recover actual damages or statutory damages of not less than $100 and not more than $5,000, plus costs and attorney's fees
  3. Obtain injunctive relief prohibiting illegal practices
  4. Cancel or waive the contract within three business days from signing, without obligation or penalty
  5. Receive a full refund of any money paid if you cancel within the three-business-day period
⚠️ Important: You cannot be obligated to pay, in advance, for credit repair services.

What Credit Repair Organizations Cannot Do

Credit repair organizations cannot:

  • Make false or misleading statements about their services
  • Charge you before delivering promised services
  • Perform any service until three business days after you sign a contract
  • Guarantee or promise any specific results
  • Advise you to dispute accurate information
  • Advise you to alter your identity to obtain credit
  • Advise you to create a new credit file

What You Should Know About Credit Repair

  • Only time, payment of debts, and responsible credit behavior can improve your credit report
  • Negative but accurate information cannot be removed from your credit report by law
  • The credit reporting agencies are required to remove accurate negative information after seven years (ten years for bankruptcy)
  • You have the right to dispute inaccurate information directly with credit reporting agencies
  • You do not need to pay anyone to exercise your rights under the Fair Credit Reporting Act

2. Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) Notice

Disclosure of Use of Consumer Reports

SCORE TO CAPITAL may obtain consumer reports and credit information about you from consumer reporting agencies to evaluate your eligibility for credit products and services, verify your identity, and assess your creditworthiness.

Your Rights Under the FCRA

You have the right to:

  1. Know what is in your file - Request your consumer report from any consumer reporting agency or visit www.annualcreditreport.com
  2. Dispute inaccurate information - Dispute directly with the consumer reporting agency and the furnisher
  3. Request corrections - Agencies must correct inaccurate information within 30 days
  4. Opt-out of prescreened offers - Call 1-888-5-OPTOUT or visit www.optoutprescreen.com
  5. Know who accessed your report - You have the right to know who requested your consumer report
  6. File a complaint - File with the Federal Trade Commission if your FCRA rights are violated

For more information: Visit www.ftc.gov/credit
