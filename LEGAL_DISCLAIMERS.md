## 1. FTC CREDIT REPAIR ORGANIZATIONS ACT (CROA) DISCLOSURE

**IMPORTANT INFORMATION ABOUT YOUR RIGHTS UNDER THE CREDIT REPAIR ORGANIZATIONS ACT**

The Credit Repair Organizations Act requires all credit repair organizations to provide you with a copy of the "Consumer Rights" statement before you are obligated to pay for credit repair services.

### YOUR CONSUMER RIGHTS

**You have the right to:**

1. **Sue a credit repair organization** that violates the Credit Repair Organizations Act or state credit repair laws within two years from the date of the violation or the date when you discovered or should have discovered the violation.

2. **Recover actual damages** or statutory damages of not less than $100 and not more than $5,000, plus the costs of the action and reasonable attorney's fees.

3. **Obtain injunctive relief**, which can prohibit credit repair organizations from engaging in illegal practices.

4. **Cancel or waive the contract** within three business days from the date you signed it or the date you received a copy of this disclosure, whichever is later, without any obligation or penalty.

5. **Receive a full refund** of any money paid to the credit repair organization if you cancel within the three-business-day period.

**You cannot be obligated to pay, in advance, for credit repair services.**

### WHAT CREDIT REPAIR ORGANIZATIONS CANNOT DO

Credit repair organizations cannot:

- Make any false or misleading statements about their services
- Charge you before delivering promised services
- Perform any service until three business days after you sign a written contract and receive a copy of this disclosure
- Guarantee or promise any specific results
- Advise you to dispute accurate information on your credit report
- Advise you to alter your identity to obtain credit
- Advise you to create a new credit file
- Make false claims about their ability to remove accurate negative information from your credit report

### WHAT YOU SHOULD KNOW ABOUT CREDIT REPAIR

- Only **time, payment of debts, and responsible credit behavior** can improve your credit report
- Negative but accurate information cannot be removed from your credit report by law
- The credit reporting agencies are required to remove accurate negative information after seven years (ten years for bankruptcy)
- You have the right to dispute inaccurate information in your credit report directly with the credit reporting agencies
- You do not need to pay anyone to exercise your rights under the Fair Credit Reporting Act

## 2. FAIR CREDIT REPORTING ACT (FCRA) NOTICE

**NOTICE REGARDING THE FAIR CREDIT REPORTING ACT**

### DISCLOSURE OF USE OF CONSUMER REPORTS

SCORE TO CAPITAL may obtain consumer reports and credit information about you from consumer reporting agencies to evaluate your eligibility for credit products and services, to verify your identity, and to assess your creditworthiness.

### YOUR RIGHTS UNDER THE FCRA

You have the right to:

1. **Know what is in your file** - You may request and obtain a copy of your consumer report from any consumer reporting agency by contacting them directly or visiting www.annualcreditreport.com for your free annual credit report.

2. **Dispute inaccurate information** - If you believe there is inaccurate or incomplete information in your consumer report, you have the right to dispute it directly with the consumer reporting agency and with the furnisher of the information.

3. **Request corrections** - Consumer reporting agencies must correct inaccurate information within 30 days of receiving a dispute.

4. **Opt-out of prescreened offers** - You can opt out of prescreened offers of credit by calling 1-888-5-OPTOUT (1-888-567-8688) or visiting www.optoutprescreen.com.

5. **Know who accessed your report** - You have the right to know who has requested your consumer report.

6. **File a complaint** - You may file a complaint with the Federal Trade Commission if you believe your rights under the FCRA have been violated.

### IMPORTANT INFORMATION

- SCORE TO CAPITAL will use your personal information only for the purposes disclosed in our Privacy Policy
- Your credit information is protected and kept confidential
- You will be notified of any significant changes to how your information is used
- You have the right to opt-out of certain uses of your information

**For more information about your rights under the FCRA, visit:** www.ftc.gov/credit
